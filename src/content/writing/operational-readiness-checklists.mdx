## Why this matters

Readiness checklists often fail because they are too generic or too long to trust under pressure.

## The structure

A useful checklist has three properties: explicit ownership, testable acceptance, and rollback clarity.

## Anti-patterns

- Vague statements that cannot be verified
- Shared ownership with no clear decider
- "Monitoring added" without alert thresholds

## What to keep

Keep the checklist short enough to run during real release windows, and strict enough to catch the common failure modes before users do.
