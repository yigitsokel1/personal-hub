# Why content-first beats page-first

Most sites start as a **page list**: home, about, blog. That works until you have more than one kind of thing to say.

## The trap

When every idea becomes a new route or a new React component, you get:

- Duplicated metadata (dates, titles, summaries)
- Inconsistent URLs and previews
- Painful refactors when navigation changes

## The alternative

Model **domains** first—projects, work, writing, labs—each with its own schema and rules. Pages become *views* over that data, not the source of truth.

## What changes in practice

You stop asking “which template?” and start asking “which **type** of content, and what fields are required?”

### Takeaway

If your hub should scale for years, invest early in **structure**. Typography and layout can follow; confused data models are expensive to unwind.
